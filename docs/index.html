Introduction

In 2013 Deepmind kickstarted the Deep RL revolution with "Playing Atari with Deep Reinforcement Learning". A seminal paper that showed that and agent could use a Deep Neural Network to determine what action to take next. This paper has been cited an astonishing 14840 times, and layed out the foundations for Deepminds successes with AlphaGo and AlphaStar. The beauty of the paper itself is its simplicity and succinctness. In the interviening 10 years the compute necessary has become far more accessible, the Deep learning frameworks have come on leaps and bounds in usability and some of the techniqueqs and architecturechoices are a little outdated. To that end I wanted to pay homage to this seminal work by recreating it PyTorch as well as spending a bit of time updating based on the current best practices. This work focuses mainly on breakout for the sake of simplicity, the algorithm for learning the control is general and can easily be applied to the other Atari games.
